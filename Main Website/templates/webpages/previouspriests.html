Parish Information

Baptism

Baptisms are held on Sundays after the 9.00 am mass. Parents have to meet the Parish Priest 6 months well in advance. Parents to attend the Pre-Baptism Course. God Parents to furnish their Baptism certificate & Pre-Baptism Course Certificate well in advance.

Reconciliation

The Priest is available on Saturdays from 6 pm to 7 pm and with prior appointment.

Communion

Once a year it’s held in our parish.

Confirmation

Once a year it's held on the last Sunday of January. (Depends on the availability of the Bishop)

Matrimony (Marriage)

Regarding marriage formalities, meet the priest on duty well in advance (at least 6 months before). Bring your latest Baptism Certificate (issued not more than six months prior).

Anointing of the Sick

Please call the Parish office or the Parish Priest on his mobile. Send someone to arrange to take the priest to the sick in hospital, nursing home, or home.

Burial

As soon as there is a death in a family, the family members or relatives have to contact the Parish Priest. He will guide you in the details of the mass and burial. We do not have our own graveyard. We have to first get permission from St. Joseph Cemetery. Copies of cause of Death Certificate from a Doctor and Burial permit from the Municipal Ward is a must.

Church Office Timings

Morning: 10AM to Noon

Evening: 5:30PM to 6:45PM

Closed on Sundays and Thursdays
